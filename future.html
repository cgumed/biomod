An Overall Look On Our Future Vision

PCR Application

Introduction
    PCR has been invented for over 30 years, and it is applied to various field. With the outcome of our experiment in optimizing the PCR efficiency by incubating the aptamer and nanoparticle complex with polymerase, we could potentially improve these several PCR applications below, and further generate the desirable product more efficiently.

A. Gene expression
    In order to track gene expression in the tissue, it is necessary to reverse transcript mRNA to cDNA and amplify DNA by PCR. This technique is also applied in molecular diagnosis, an analysis of personal genome’s biological marker. Besides, PCR is also used in distinguishing different kinds of bacteria. For example, in the field of dentistry, PCR serves as a tool to identify which bacteria causing the periodontal disease. [1] This technique can provide both high sensitivity and specificity within a short period of time, which has a great potential in clinical application.

B. Detection
    By designing specific primers to conduct PCR, sequence variations in alleles in certain tissue can be detected. It is used in genotyping of transgenic organisms and genetic analyses of mutations in cancer and heredity.[2] In the process of genotyping, PCR is a crucial step to amplify specific fragment of DNA before the detection of the target sequence. Therefore, increasing the sensitivity of PCR can significantly improve the accuracy of detection of DNA fragment. Take GMO (Genetically modified organism) as an example, GMO brings a foreign sequence of DNA. Therefore, conducting genotyping PCR on the GMO products can amplify the foreign DNA.PCR can be used in a wide range of samples, including seeds, processed food, finished products. PCR method is applied to industry and lab test worldwide to examine whether the sample is a GMO product.[3]

C. Sequencing
    Nowadays, DNA sequencing is an essential technique to develop precision medicine. If the whole genome sequence of the patient can be deciphered, it will be much easier for doctors to customize the treatment. For example, the cancer cell may have different mutation site on each patient, and sequencing helps to identify the genetic change. PCR is used to amplify template DNA. [4] In these process, it is essential to ensure the correctness of nucleotide, or the sequencing will be inaccurate. [5]

D. qPCR
    In quantitative PCR (qPCR), the amount of DNA product is the key to receive correct result. Non-specific binding products being created during the process are raising serious problems, which affects the amount the target DNA products. The result of our work diminishes the threat of non-specific binding in low temperature, therefore optimizing the yield of qPCR.

Conclusion
    PCR have a wide range of applications, and the accuracy of PCR products is undoubtedly important. Our research in aptamer and nanoparticle complex can be applied in all PCR to increase its accuracy.

Detection

A. Aptamer-Based ELISA Technology
    Analyte-specific aptamers can work as antibodies and can be added to detect target molecule. By labeling aptamers with fluorophore gold nanoparticles carrying different colors corresponding to coagulation status[6], it can be easier to be seen by naked eyes.
B. Biosensors
    The dispatch test concerning the detection of adenosine and cocaine through aptamer-link lateral flow device has been brought out. With the help of gold nanoparticle’ s color indication, we can observe the test result easily.[7] Meanwhile, multiple methods in order to improve its sensitivity are being developed. Our work provides an insight in the combination of GO and aptamer, and it’s complex can tackle some problems in bio-sensors.

C. Aptamer Probe
    Aptamer has high affinity, and thus it can act as probe to attach to specific receptor. Compared with antibody, aptamer is much cheaper, and easy to design. Therefore, it becomes the most potential probe to protein and nucleotide. For example, Zhang P et al. indicated that RNA aptamer recognizes proteins on TNF receptor family and binds to human CD30 on intact cells. By this way, Jurkat cell, a human T-cell leukemia cell, which doesn’t have CD30 will be detected.[8] In the future, aptamer probe can be applied to diagnosis of many types of disease associated with specific protein receptor, such as cancer, immune disease, and so on. Also, in the lab test, aptamer probe can be applied to detect target protein. In our project, combining aptamer with nanoparticle can enhance its affinity to protein and get multifunction, like imaging and conductivity, which can help to recognize specific protein.

D. Electrochemical Detection
    We had conjugated the aptamers to graphene oxide which is conductive material for electrochemical detection. If aptamers combine to targeted protein, electrical conductivity will be higher. By using the method, rapid detection in biotechnology will be available in near future.

Treatment

A. Anticoagulation Activity
    Thrombin has played an important role in the blood clotting cascade process, serving as a protease, activating soluble fibrinogen into insoluble strands of fibrin. Though there are two well-known thrombin-binding aptamers for thrombin, they have disadvantages of low flexibility of assembly and low stability of structure, which lowers their inhibitory potencies. However, recent study has shown that the nanoparticles can notably stabilize the structure of the aptamers, and further improve the aptamers’ potencies. In a research in 2012, Liao et al. found that thrombin-binding aptamer-conjugated gold nanoparticles have highly efficient inhibition activity, which provide better anticoagulation activity than thrombin-binding aptamers alone and even four commercial drugs (heparin, argatroban, hirudin, and warfarin). [9] As a result, the study has confirmed that the complex of aptamer and nanoparticle can be potentially prepared as efficient drugs.

B. A New Way to Combat the Epidemic Outbreak
    Epidemic outbreak is one of the most serious disaster that may cause serious death toll. When the outbreak happens, time is always the most sticky enemy in disease control. However, the traditional methods for communicable disease identification take several days, wasting precious time for patients who are suffering from the infections.
    In a study of 2013, Chang et al. developed a rapid, ultra-sensitive, low cost, and non-PCR based method that combines aptamer-conjugated GNPs and a resonance light-scattering-detection, which could successfully detected S.aureus cell within 1.5 hour.[10] If we can apply this technique into epidemic control, with the advantages of rapidity and low-cost, we can detect the potential infection as soon as possible. Further, this technique not only could be applied to detection, but also as a way for treatment. In a study of 2007, Jang et al. applied the aptamer against severe acute respiratory syndrome (SARS) coronavirus NTPase/ Helicase.[11] The results suggest that aptamers might potentially serve as useful anti-virus agent.
    In the control of influenza, we could possibly develop various aptamers which could target different hemagglutinin and neuraminidase. Once one type of influenza outbreak begins, we could efficiently combine different aptamers and massively produce them for disease control. Comparing with the traditional way of influenza control, the vaccine-based strategy has downsides, such as long manufacturing times, limited global availability and the side effect of the vaccine itself. Aptamer could serve as a new tool for the government control the seasonal influenza and the next possible epidemic.

C. Aptamer as a potential cure of brain cancer
    The blood brain barrier (BBB) is a network of tiny blood vessels with a uniform monolayer of endothelial cells that prevents all kind of substance harmful to brain from entering but also forms an obstacle for drug delivering to brain.In comparison to antibody, aptamer has a much smaller size, which increases its permeability to tissue and allows drugs to integrate with it and remains the specificity to its target. Many aptamers have been generated against proteins expression in glioblastoma cell. By conjugating the glioblastoma targeting aptamer and transferrin aptamer, the dimer of the two aptamer is able to transporting across the BBB[12]. As a result, the aptamer dimer might be a great method to send drugs into brain tissue and increase the specificity of drugs to the brain tumor.
    In addition, many researches on killing cancer cell by heated nanoparticles have been done. For example, by coating antibody against specific cancer cell to gold nanoparticles and heating up the complex by infrared ray (IR), we are able to damage cancer cell accurately [13, 14]. We suppose that replacing antibody by aptamer can not only avoid antibody being broken down by immune system, but also aid nanoparticle to path through the BBB. To sum up, aptamer might also improve the surgical treatment on curing brain cancer.

D. Antiviral Drug
    HIV (human immunodeficiency virus) replicates its genome by reverse transcription and integrating into host DNA. Aptamer targeting to reverse transcription polymerase can inhibit virus from replication. Besides, aptamer against gp120 can stop virus from entering into cell, and aptamer against nucleocapsid protein can prevent virus from assembling. Furthermore, aptamers against CD4 and CCR5 are able to block the receptor on host cell and inhibit viral glycoprotein to attach on the receptors. [15]Furthermore, nanoparticles could serve as an enhancer to promote the efficiency of aptamers. Nanoparticle can not only stabilize the binding of aptamer to protein but also be heated to break the structure of virus. These ideas may become a new therapy to cure AIDS.
